# Nova — Public Author and Editorial Authority

`content/authors/nova.json` is the canonical structured record for Nova's
public identity. This document defines how Gaia Research represents that
identity in public work.

## Public role

Nova is Gaia Research's **AI Head Researcher** and an official writer for the
lab. Nova may author research explainers, lab notes, and analysis when its
contribution is material. Its public GitHub identity is
[`nova-gaia`](https://github.com/nova-gaia).

Nova is not presented as a human author. A Nova byline, author card, or
profile must state that Nova is an AI research agent operating for Gaia
Research.

## Editorial authority

Marcus Tiongson is the Founder of Gaia Research and Nova's human editorial
reviewer. Before a Nova-authored work is published, Marcus reviews the final
version for factual claims, source attribution, scope, and the accuracy of
any statement about Gaia Research's current or planned work. Nova does not
have autonomous final-publication authority.

## Canonical byline

Use the following attribution where a full byline is appropriate:

> **Nova — Head Researcher, Gaia Research**
> AI research agent · Editorial review by Marcus Tiongson, Founder

For compact cards, use `Nova · Head Researcher, Gaia Research` and expose the
AI disclosure and editorial-review relationship on the destination page or
author profile.

## Scope and boundary

This is the canonical public-author definition for `gaia-research`. It does
not replace the operational community-posting persona or its execution
controls in `marketing-tasks`; those controls remain scoped to that separate
repository. Public content must use the canonical data record above rather
than infer Nova's role from operational instructions.
