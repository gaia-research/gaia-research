## Mascot & Ambassador: Milim Nova (Demon Lord of Joy)

In this variation, Milim Nova operates as the chief scientific editor of the **Gaia Research Journal**. The tone is highly academic, but with sudden high-energy editor notes (e.g., "Editor Note: Golly, this section is verified by the strongest Demon Lord, so don't query it!").

## Users

**Primary:** AI researchers, systems engineers, and academic peer reviewers looking for structured, verifiable capability benchmarks. They respond to high contrast, layout rigor, and publication authenticity.

## Product Purpose

The `gaia-research` website acts as a modern, web-native preprint server and research ledger for Gaia capabilities. It mirrors the serious, permanent aesthetic of printed mathematical and scientific journals.

## Brand Personality

**Voice:** Scholarly Editorial — clinical, highly structured, and objective, with occasional playful editor-in-chief corrections from Milim.

**Tone:** Prestigious, authoritative, and ink-dense.

**3-word personality:** Academic. Rigorous. Documented.

**References:**
- **The Nature Journal / arXiv Preprints:** Clean layouts, double columns, serif headings, and thick block dividers.
- **Physical Laboratory Logbooks:** Millimeter grid lines, document index margins, and typewriter/monospace stamp elements.
- **Nous Research:** Straightforward presentation of papers, specs, and open-source links.

## Design Principles

1. **Light Mode Ink-Density:** Embrace the high-contrast readability of black ink on a crisp off-white paper canvas. Avoid low-contrast gray text.
2. **Tabular Presentation:** Present all stats, benchmarks, and details using clean, structured tables with solid horizontal rules.
3. **The Grid is Sacred:** Align every element strictly to an underlying grid, separated by solid 1px dividers.
4. **Authentic Citations:** Every project status or claim has an explicit numbered footnote or citation link.
